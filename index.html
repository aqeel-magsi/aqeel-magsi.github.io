Aqeel Abbas

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Shenzhen Research Institute, The Chinese University of Hong Kong. I completed my Ph.D. in Earthquake Seismology at The Chinese University of Hong Kong in September 2024. My research focuses on induced seismicity, waveform-based earthquake analysis, seismic attenuation, ambient-noise interferometry, and clock-drift inspection and correction for temporary seismic arrays. My current work focuses on fluid-induced earthquakes and dense-array observations in the Sichuan Basin, China.

More information is available in my resume, Google Scholar, and LinkedIn profiles.

Research Projects

Induced Earthquakes and Fluid-Driven Seismicity

Hydraulic fracturing can change local earthquake productivity and provides an opportunity to image crustal heterogeneity, reservoir structure, and fluid-injection effects. My work combines dense-array waveform analysis, high-resolution earthquake catalogs, relocation, attenuation measurements, and waveform-based indicators to understand induced seismicity in the Weiyuan shale gas field, Sichuan Basin.

Clock-Drift Inspection and Correction in Seismic Data

Temporary seismic deployments are valuable for earthquake monitoring, but timing problems can reduce the reliability of waveform analysis. I use local earthquakes, teleseismic waveforms, and ambient-noise cross-correlation functions to diagnose and correct timing offsets in land and ocean-bottom seismic data. This work supports robust earthquake detection, association, relocation, and waveform interpretation.

Ocean-Bottom Seismic Data Processing and Crustal Structure

I have worked on ocean-bottom seismic and multichannel seismic datasets from the northern South China Sea to study lithospheric rifting, transitional crust, and lower-crustal high-velocity structures using forward ray tracing and travel-time tomography.
